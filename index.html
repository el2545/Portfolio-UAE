01 — UAE Policy Context

UAE Policy Context: Climate, Mobility, and Data Governance.

The UAE setting is treated as an empirical constraint environment. Extreme heat, limited freshwater, land-use pressure, carbon-transition obligations, high-density logistics, and rapid infrastructure digitization are framed as policy-evaluation constraints rather than visual branding.

Climate as Climate-Aware Design

Traditional wind towers are treated as a policy metaphor: good urban systems reduce energy demand before adding technology. The portfolio therefore links return, carbon exposure, and resilience.

Resource Scarcity and Trade as Risk Logic

04 — Identification & Appraisal & Policy Evaluation

Methods layer: forecasting, MCDA, and causal-evaluation discipline.

Modelling is presented as an appraisal and transparency layer. Rankings, forecasts, and simulations must expose inputs, assumptions, weights, sensitivity, and limits before they are translated into policy language.

Evidence Inputs

Traffic indicators, GIS exposure, cost structures, emissions proxies, demand signals, and regulatory constraints.

Forecasting Layer

XGBoost is treated as a forecasting tool only; prediction performance is not interpreted as causal evidence.

Appraisal Layer

MCDA/AROMAN, NPV, BCR, and scenario analysis support ranking, robustness checks, and sensitivity reporting.

Governance Layer

Data minimization, purpose limitation, source logs, model documentation, human review, and claim downgrading when data cannot sustain causal identification.

Data-governance protocol: the research design applies data minimization, purpose limitation, source logging, anonymization or aggregation where feasible, retention limits, access control, and documented treatment of cross-border processing risks. AI-assisted components will be accompanied by model-card style documentation, assumption logs, sensitivity analysis, and human review. Ethical claims will be restricted to what the available data, institutional approvals, and UAE legal framework can support.
